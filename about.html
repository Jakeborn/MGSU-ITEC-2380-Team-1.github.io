About Us

Learn more about our mission, history, and the team behind Thank You, Fur Your Service

Our Mission Statement

At Thank You, Fur Your Service, we believe that no veteran should face the challenges of post-service life alone. Our mission is to provide highly trained service animals to veterans in need, offering both practical assistance and emotional support to help them regain independence and improve their quality of life.

What We Do

We carefully match veterans with service animals trained to assist with specific needs, including PTSD support, mobility assistance, and medical alert capabilities. Each animal undergoes rigorous training before being placed with a veteran.

Why It Matters

Service animals can transform lives, helping veterans with physical disabilities regain mobility or providing emotional grounding for those struggling with PTSD. Our work bridges the gap between military service and civilian life.

Our Story

Thank You, Fur Your Service was founded in 2015 by Marine Corps veteran Daniel Johnson and his service dog, Ranger. After experiencing firsthand the life-changing impact of a service animal, Daniel made it his mission to help other veterans access this vital resource.

2015: Humble Beginnings

Started with just Daniel and Ranger, training one service dog at a time in a small facility in Warner Robins, GA.

2017: Non-Profit Status

Received 501(c)(3) status, allowing us to expand our services and accept tax-deductible donations.

2020: New Facility

Moved to our current 5-acre training facility, allowing us to train up to 15 service animals simultaneously.

Today

We've placed over 200 service animals with veterans across Georgia and are expanding our reach to neighboring states.

Frequently Asked Questions

We primarily train dogs to assist with PTSD, mobility issues, and medical alert needs. All our animals undergo specialized training based on the veteran's specific requirements.

Our training program typically takes 12-18 months, depending on the specific skills needed. This includes basic obedience, specialized task training, and public access training.

Thanks to generous donors, we provide service animals at no cost to qualified veterans. We do ask veterans to cover basic care items like food and veterinary visits after placement.

Service animals are trained to perform specific tasks to assist with disabilities and have public access rights under the ADA. Emotional support animals provide comfort but don't have specialized training or the same legal protections.

We welcome volunteers for puppy socialization, administrative help, fundraising events, and more. Visit our Contact page to fill out a volunteer application.
